NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE ADMINISTRATION

APOLLO 11 TECHNICAL AIR-TO-GROUND VOICE TRANSCRIPTION

Prepared by Test Division
Apollo Spacecraft Program Office

MANNED SPACECRAFT CENTER
HOUSTON, TEXAS
August 1969

INTRODUCTION

This is the transcription of the technical air-to-ground voice communications of the Apollo 11 mission. The transcript covers communications from launch through splashdown.

CREW IDENTIFICATIONS:
CDR - Neil A. Armstrong, Commander
CMP - Michael Collins, Command Module Pilot
LMP - Edwin E. Aldrin Jr., Lunar Module Pilot
CC - Capsule Communicator (CapCom)

=== LAUNCH SEQUENCE ===

000:00:00 CC: Ten, nine, eight, seven, six, five, four, three, two, one, zero. All engines running. LIFTOFF! We have a liftoff, 32 minutes past the hour. Liftoff on Apollo 11.

000:00:13 CDR: Roll program.
000:00:14 CC: Roger. Roll.
000:00:30 CDR: Roll complete and pitch is programmed.

000:01:02 CC: Apollo 11, this is Houston. You are GO for staging.
000:01:05 CDR: Roger. Go for staging.

000:02:44 CDR: Staging. And ignition.
000:02:47 CC: We confirm ignition, and you're looking good.

000:09:08 CC: Apollo 11, you are GO for orbit.
000:09:10 CDR: Roger, Houston. We're showing 103 by 103.

=== TRANSLUNAR INJECTION ===

002:44:16 CC: Apollo 11, this is Houston. You are GO for TLI.
002:44:19 CDR: Roger. Apollo 11, GO for TLI.

002:50:13 CDR: Ignition.
002:55:42 CDR: Shutdown.
002:55:45 CC: We confirm shutdown. You're on your way to the Moon.

=== LUNAR ORBIT INSERTION ===

075:49:50 CDR: Roger. Ignition.
075:55:53 CDR: Shutdown.
075:55:56 CC: Welcome to lunar orbit, gentlemen.

=== DESCENT TO LUNAR SURFACE ===

102:33:05 CC: Eagle, Houston. You're GO for powered descent.
102:33:08 CDR: Roger. Understand. GO for powered descent.

102:38:22 LMP: Program alarm.
102:38:24 CDR: It's a 1202.
102:38:32 CC: Roger. We got you. We're GO on that alarm.

102:42:17 LMP: 35 degrees. 35 degrees. 750, coming down at 23. 700 feet, 21 down. 400 feet, down 9. Got the shadow out there. 250, down 2Â½. Picking up some dust. 4 forward. 4 forward. Drifting to the right a little.

102:45:40 LMP: Contact light.
102:45:43 CDR: Shutdown.
102:45:44 LMP: Okay. Engine stop.

102:45:57 CDR: Houston, Tranquility Base here. The Eagle has landed.
102:46:06 CC: Roger, Tranquility. We copy you on the ground. You got a bunch of guys about to turn blue. We're breathing again. Thanks a lot.

=== FIRST LUNAR STEPS ===

109:24:15 CDR: I'm at the foot of the ladder. The LM footpads are only depressed in the surface about 1 or 2 inches. Although the surface appears to be very, very fine-grained as you get close to it, it's almost like a powder.

109:24:48 CDR: I'm going to step off the LM now.

109:24:48 CDR: That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.

109:25:08 CDR: Yes, the surface is fine and powdery. I can pick it up loosely with my toe. It does adhere in fine layers, like powdered charcoal, to the sole and sides of my boots.

109:43:16 LMP: Beautiful view!
109:43:18 CDR: Isn't that something? Magnificent sight out here.
109:43:24 LMP: Magnificent desolation.

=== FLAG DEPLOYMENT ===

110:10:33 CDR: We're going to try to position the flag now.
110:16:13 CC: Neil, this is Houston. The President of the United States is in his office now and would like to say a few words to you.
110:16:19 CDR: That would be an honor.

110:16:30 PRESIDENT NIXON: Neil and Buzz, I am talking to you by telephone from the Oval Room at the White House. And this certainly has to be the most historic telephone call ever made.

=== LUNAR LIFTOFF ===

124:22:00 CDR: Roger. 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, abort stage, engine arm ascent, proceed.
124:22:01 LMP: Proceed. Beautiful!
124:22:04 CC: Roger. Looking good here.
124:22:07 CDR: We're off! Look at that stuff go all over the place!

=== RENDEZVOUS ===

128:03:00 CDR: We're station-keeping on the Columbia.
128:03:04 CC: Roger. Standing by for hard dock.

128:12:00 CDR: That was a funny one. You know, I didn't feel it strike, and then I thought things were pretty steady. I went boom with it, and that time it really caught it. It's captured.
128:12:08 CC: We copy. It's been a mighty fine day.

=== TRANS-EARTH INJECTION ===

135:23:42 CC: Apollo 11, you're GO for TEI.
135:23:45 CDR: Roger. GO for TEI.

=== REENTRY ===

194:49:17 CC: Apollo 11, you're looking very good at this distance.
194:49:21 CDR: Thank you.

195:12:06 CC: Apollo 11, this is Hornet. We have you in sight.
195:18:35 CDR: Splashdown!

195:18:42 CC: Apollo 11, Hornet. The swimmers are on the way. Congratulations on a great flight!

END OF TRANSCRIPT
